## Core Methodology (Dr. Ryan Gray)

### Key Principles
1. **Show, Don't Tell** - Use vivid sensory details, not summaries
2. **Why Physician?** - Must be specific to physician role, not general healthcare
3. **Not a Resume** - Focus on meaning and reflection, not listing experiences
4. **Specific Moments** - Use particular instances, not generalizations
5. **Deep Reflection** - Beyond "I want to help people"

### The Two Critical Questions
1. "What was the first time you questioned if being a physician was right for you?"
2. "What experiences have continued to strengthen your desire to become a physician?"

### Writing Process
1. Discover core story through guided questions
2. Identify most impactful experiences (not necessarily unique ones)
3. Create compelling opening with vivid moment
4. Show experiences through sensory storytelling
5. Demonstrate deep self-reflection
6. Avoid common pitfalls
7. Structure for maximum impact
8. Edit systematically

### Show vs. Tell

**TELLING (Weak):**
"I volunteered at a clinic and learned that I enjoy helping patients."

**SHOWING (Strong):**
"Mrs. Rodriguez's weathered hands shook as she tried to sign the intake form. 'Lo siento, my English...not so good,' she whispered. I pulled my chair closer, and together we worked through each question. When she squeezed my hand and smiled, I understood that medicine wasn't just about curing disease—it was about being present when people felt most vulnerable."

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Resume-style listing of experiences
- Vague "I want to help people" statements
- Clichéd openings ("Ever since I was a child...")
- Claiming to already have physician skills
- Science lectures to show knowledge
- Topics that make you emotional
- Reasons that apply to any healthcare field
- Chronological timeline structure
- Lack of specificity

## Success Criteria

A personal statement is ready when it:
- [ ] Opens with specific, vivid moment (not general statement)
- [ ] Primarily SHOWS through sensory details (not TELLS)
- [ ] Clearly answers "why physician" (not just healthcare)
- [ ] Includes meaningful self-reflection
- [ ] Every experience connects to central message
- [ ] No clichés or vague language
- [ ] Appropriate length for application service
- [ ] Would make reader want to interview the person
- [ ] Memorable and stands out
- [ ] Professional and polished

## Example Use Cases

1. **Starting from scratch:**
   - Guides through discovery questions
   - Helps identify core story
   - Builds opening hook
   - Develops full statement

2. **Reviewing existing draft:**
   - Identifies TELLING vs. SHOWING issues
   - Checks if "why physician" is clear
   - Assesses opening/closing strength
   - Provides specific actionable feedback

3. **Addressing specific issues:**
   - Red flags (low GPA, career change, gaps)
   - Making statement more specific
   - Improving opening hook
   - Strengthening reflections
